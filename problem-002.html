The Problem

In the figure, A is the center of the circle. Points B, C, and D lie on the circle. Points B, J, and F are collinear with BJ = JF. In triangle HJI: JI = 2.5, HI = 1.5, and HJ = 2.

Find AC.

// Figure
(a)
Part (a)

Identify the Right Angle

Show that triangle HJI contains a right angle. State clearly which angle is the right angle and justify your answer using the Pythagorean theorem.

(b)
Part (b)

Determine BJ

Using the result from part (a) and the given condition that B, J, F are collinear with BJ = JF, determine the length BJ. Show your reasoning clearly.

(c)
Part (c)

Find AC

CB is a diameter of the circle and A is its center — meaning A is the midpoint of CB. Using your results from parts (a) and (b), find the length AC.
