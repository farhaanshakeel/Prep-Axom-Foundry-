Problem #001

Subject
Physics — Mechanics
Topics
Free Body Diagram · Kinematics · Differential Equations · Optimization
Level
Grade 11–12 / Olympiad Foundational
Proposed By
PAF Founding Team
The Problem

A ball of mass m is thrown vertically upward from the ground with an initial velocity u. Unlike ideal conditions, the surrounding air exerts a resistive force on the ball that is directly proportional to its instantaneous velocity. This resistive force is given by f = mkv, where k is a positive constant and v is the velocity of the ball at any instant.

Take the upward direction as positive. Acceleration due to gravity is g.

(a)
Part (a)

Free Body Diagram

Draw a clearly labelled Free Body Diagram of the ball during its upward journey. Hence, using Newton's Second Law, write the equation of motion of the ball.

(b)
Part (b)

Time To Maximum Height

Using the equation of motion from part (a), determine the time T taken by the ball to reach its maximum height.

(c)
Part (c)

Maximum Height With Air Resistance

Show that the maximum height H reached by the ball is given by:

H = u/k − g/k² · ln((ku + g)/g)
(d)
Part (d)

Height Loss Optimization

In the absence of air resistance, the maximum height reached by the ball is:

H₀ = u²/2g

Define the height loss due to air resistance as ΔH(k) = H₀ − H(k). Using differentiation, find the value of k for which the rate of change of height loss with respect to k is zero. Interpret your answer physically.
